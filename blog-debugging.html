Debugging Like A Detective.

There's a specific feeling you get when a bug only happens in production, intermittently, on a Tuesday, when it's raining. It's a mixture of dread and pure intrigue. Over the years, I've realized that debugging isn't just about reading stack traces—it's essentially a game of Clue.

Was it the state manager in the billing module with a race condition? Or was it the third-party API that silently changed its schema?

The Method

1. Don't trust the obvious. Often, the error message thrown is just a side effect of the real issue that occurred three functions earlier. Always walk backward.

2. Isolate the crime scene. If you can't reproduce the bug, you can't fix it. Spend 90% of your time trying to figure out exactly how to trigger the issue consistently.

3. Read the logs like a novel. Logs are the testimonies of your system's components. They might be lying, or they might be omitting the truth, but they are your best witnesses.

The Fix

Once you've figured out the "who, what, and where," the fix is usually a single line of code. It's almost anticlimactic. But the rush of finally closing that ticket? That's why we do this job.
